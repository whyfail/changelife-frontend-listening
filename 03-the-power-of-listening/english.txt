Hello, I'm Emma. I want to share a small habit that changed how I learn English.

Every day I listen to simple English for just ten minutes while I have my morning tea.

The words are easy, the sentences are short, and the stories are about daily life.

After a few days, I start recognizing phrases without trying, like greeting someone or ordering a drink.

It feels like the language is slowly becoming a part of my everyday world.

I also listen while walking to school or waiting for the bus, using free time to learn something new.

At first I had to pause often, but now I can follow the main ideas without stopping.

My pronunciation improves too, because I hear the correct sounds and repeat them softly.

I keep a small notebook where I write new words I hear, then I practice them later.

When I meet a friend, I try to use a short phrase I learned, and it feels wonderful.

The more I listen, the more natural English sounds, like a familiar song I love.

I no longer feel nervous when I hear a conversation; I feel curious and ready to join.

If you give yourself even a few minutes of simple listening each day, you will see progress.

Keep listening, and you will watch your confidence grow, one gentle sentence at a time.
