When the day is over, I give my mind a gentle sign that it is time to rest at home.

First, I put my phone away and make the room a little darker, softer, and quieter.

The soft light helps my eyes relax, and the quiet tells my body that the busy day is ending.

I wash my face, brush my teeth, and change into comfortable clothes without trying to hurry.

Then I sit for a few quiet minutes with a simple book or some calm music.

I do not choose anything exciting because I want my thoughts to slow down before bed.

If a worry comes to mind, I write it on a small piece of paper for tomorrow.

This simple action lets me stop carrying that worry around in my head all night.

Next, I take a slow breath in and an even slower breath out through my mouth.

With each breath, my shoulders feel softer, and my whole body becomes a little heavier.

I think about one good thing from the day, even if it was something very small.

Maybe I enjoyed a warm meal, heard a kind word, or saw a beautiful evening sky.

These quiet thoughts help me feel thankful, safe, and ready to close my eyes and sleep.

A peaceful night begins with a few gentle choices, and everything else can wait until tomorrow morning.
